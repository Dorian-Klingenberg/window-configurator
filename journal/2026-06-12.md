## Sandbox 002 — idea-refine

The skill ran against the confirmed intent from Sandbox 001. Eight variations generated in Phase 1; two rejected immediately on hard constraints:

- Image processing for final measurements: 3/32" accuracy requires physical interior remeasure after pulling casing. Non-negotiable.
- Pipeline dashboard: the contractor's CRM handles this. Out of system boundary.

The rejections are as valuable as the accepted variations — they produced the first two entries in the "Not Doing" list and confirmed the physical remeasure requirement as absolute.

Phase 2 clustered the remaining six into three directions. The key insight: Directions A (contractor on-site efficiency) and B (customer self-service) are not competing alternatives. They are complementary halves. A without B still leaves the contractor explaining grille patterns on the phone. B without A still wastes the contractor's on-site time. Both are required for the system to actually save the contractor installation time.

Direction C (customer as first photographer) is real but fragile — customer photo quality is unpredictable and a poor estimate damages trust in pricing. Named as a Phase 2 feature, not MVP.

A critical NFR pair arrived between phases: performance (no per-change server round-trip; existing tools are slow and it kills engagement) and usability (no window expertise required; existing tools are expert-only). Both are added to ConOps constraints and Mission Objectives needs (N-10, N-11).
